What can they tell us about genre? Taking a dataset from Kaggle scaped from IMDb, whittled down to english language films with full data entries left 3651 films. Let's see what they have to say about genre, make sure to hover over the data to let it speak.

This bar not only shows the interesting split of genres through films, but also shows the sample size for each genre. The data for drama is likely to be more accurate in the following graphs than western because of the bigger sample size.

Though animation and family both seem to be good ways to return a good ticket gross from your budget investment, horror it seems is one of the best genres to invest in. On ticket sales alone, the data suggests it provides a similiar return once the budget has been accounted for. The data here is perhaps misleading though. The film industry doesn't make all its money from ticket sales alone, particularly with family and animated films. With DVD sales, and sales of merchandise, the actual profit made goes beyond the gross ticket sales shown in the graph. Animated and family films are likely to make a lot more money than horror, even after budget is accounted for.

This chart recognises a number of different trends in the film industry between 1980 and 2016. Notably the rise of animated films, with computer animation meaning that animated films are becoming steadily cheaper to produce. This has allowed the rise of animated film companies other than Disney to become big players in the industry, for example the rise of DreamWorks in 1997, which stemmed from the success of Amblimation's films in the early 90s.

One of the other interesting trends is the boom of horror and sci-fi films in the 80s. The increasing technology allowed for better special effects, aiding both genres. The Sci-Fi genre recieved E.T. and two more additions to the Star Wars franchise, sparking the imagination for Sci-Fi films. While the Horror genre continued growing strong from it's boom in the 70s, recieving sequels from many films like Halloween and new entries to the genre such as The Shining and Gremlins.

The chord diagram above shows some interesting trends when hovering to see how many films link each genre. While Romance is linked to all of the genres by some degree, its links to comedy and drama are much greater than to any of the other genres. As seen by the size of the arcs, Drama and Comedy have the greatest links to other genres. Drama and Comedy are most likely to be sub-genres to other genres, as they dictate the tone of the film and not just the setting, like fantasy and war does. The chord diagram gives greater understanding to the graphs above, the budget for a genre will vary depending on what other genres it is linked to. For example a comedy linked to an animation is likely to have a higher budget than one linked to a romance.
